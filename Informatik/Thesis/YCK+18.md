# Byzantine-Robust Distributed Learning: Towards Optimal Statistical Rates
#literature

## Summary
The paper focuses on developing distributed statistical learning algorithms that are robust against "Byzantine failures," which refer to arbitrary and potentially adversarial behavior from some computing units in a decentralized environment. The authors aim to achieve both optimal statistical performance and high communication efficiency.

## Algorithms
- Median-based Gradient Descent
- Trimmed-mean-based Gradient Descent

## Simulations
Malicious nodes performed a [[Label Flipping]] attack on the [[MNIST]] dataset. The results were compared against a baseline without mitigations.
